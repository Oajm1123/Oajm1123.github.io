About Me

I’m a third-year Mechanical Engineering and Applied Mathematics student at Queen’s University with a passion for designing, analyzing, and building practical solutions to real-world problems. My experience spans CAD modeling, prototyping, simulation, and programming, including leading hands-on projects such as a robotic arm, an autonomous drone concept, and a brachistochrone-modeled rollercoaster. I enjoy working on interdisciplinary teams, managing timelines, and iterating through design challenges—from mechanical design and manufacturing to embedded systems and automation. I'm continuously developing my technical and communication skills, and I'm excited to apply them in a wide range of mechanical engineering roles.

  • Proficient in SolidWorks, MATLAB, Arduino, and Python
  • Experienced in mechanical design, simulation, and prototyping
  • Led and contributed to engineering teams on multi-system projects

Experience

Queen's University

Teaching Assistant

I was a teaching assistant at Queen's University for both the first year engineering calculus class and the first year engineering linear algebra. My responsibilities in this role included meeting once a week with the professor and fellow teaching assistants to discuss the curriculum for the week and preparing and delivering a one hour tutorial in which I would help the students go through practice problems, study for their upcoming tests, and answer any questions they had about the material covered in the lectures during the week.

Appalachian Land Management

Forest Technician

I have worked at Appalachian Land Management for two summers as a Forest Technician, where I collaborated with a team to complete environmental restoration projects focused on removing invasive plant species from state game lands. For the past five months, I have served as a team lead. In this role, I am responsible for ensuring safety protocols are followed, training team members for daily field tasks, managing and accounting for weekly equipment needs, and troubleshooting on-site challenges such as equipment malfunctions or difficult terrain.
